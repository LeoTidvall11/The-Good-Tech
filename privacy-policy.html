Privacy Policy – The Good Tech

Last updated: April 10, 2026  ·  Effective date: April 10, 2026

1. Introduction

Welcome to The Good Tech ("we", "us", "our"), a news platform dedicated to reporting on technology, innovation, and digital trends with a constructive and forward-looking perspective. We believe that good technology deserves good journalism — and that good journalism starts with respecting the people who read it.

This Privacy Policy explains what personal data we collect when you visit our website (thegoodtech.com), how we use it, who we share it with, how long we keep it, and what rights you have regarding your personal information. It applies to all services, features, and content provided by The Good Tech across our website, mobile experience, and newsletter.

We encourage you to read this policy carefully. By using our website or subscribing to our newsletter, you acknowledge that you have read and understood how we handle your personal data in accordance with this policy.

5. Cookies and Similar Technologies

5.1 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They allow the website to remember information about your visit — such as your preferences or login status — and help us understand how our site is being used.

5.2 The Cookies We Use

Strictly necessary cookies (always active)

These cookies are essential for the website to function and cannot be switched off. They store no personally identifiable data and are used for purposes such as:

  • Storing your cookie consent decision
  • Maintaining your login session
  • Protecting forms from automated spam (CSRF protection)

Functional cookies

These allow us to offer enhanced functionality and personalisation, such as:

  • Remembering your preferred language
  • Storing your display preferences (e.g. dark mode, font size)
  • Keeping you logged in across sessions

Analytics cookies

These help us understand how visitors interact with our site, including:

  • Google Analytics 4 (with IP anonymisation enabled)
  • Scroll depth and heatmap tracking
  • A/B testing for layout and feature improvements

Marketing and advertising cookies

Used to serve you relevant advertisements on our site and across third-party platforms. These cookies are only activated with your explicit consent.

5.3 Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You can manage your cookie choices at any time using the Cookie Settings panel accessible via the link in the footer of every page. You may also configure your browser to block or alert you to cookies. Please note that blocking strictly necessary cookies may cause parts of the website to stop functioning correctly.

Some of our service providers are based outside the United Kingdom or the European Economic Area (EEA). Where your personal data is transferred to countries not deemed to provide an adequate level of protection, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, including:

You may request a copy of the safeguards we rely on for international transfers by contacting us at privacy@thegoodtech.com.

9. Your Rights

Under the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018, you have the following rights regarding your personal data. We aim to respond to all requests within 30 calendar days.

9.1 Right of Access (Article 15)

You have the right to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you, along with information about how we process it.

9.2 Right to Rectification (Article 16)

If the data we hold about you is inaccurate or incomplete, you have the right to ask us to correct or complete it.

9.3 Right to Erasure — "Right to be Forgotten" (Article 17)

You have the right to request that we delete your personal data in certain circumstances, for example:

  • The data is no longer necessary for the purpose it was collected
  • You withdraw your consent and there is no other lawful basis for processing
  • You object to processing and there are no overriding legitimate grounds
  • The data has been unlawfully processed

Note that the right to erasure is not absolute and may not apply where processing is necessary to comply with a legal obligation.

9.4 Right to Restriction of Processing (Article 18)

You have the right to request that we restrict how we process your data — for example, while we investigate a rectification request or an objection.

9.5 Right to Data Portability (Article 20)

Where processing is based on your consent or a contract, and is carried out by automated means, you have the right to receive your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format, and to transmit it to another controller.

9.6 Right to Object (Article 21)

You have the right to object at any time to processing based on legitimate interests, including profiling for direct marketing purposes. If you object to direct marketing, we will stop immediately.

9.7 Right to Withdraw Consent

Where processing is based on your consent, you may withdraw it at any time. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

9.8 Right to Lodge a Complaint

If you believe we have not handled your personal data correctly, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the UK supervisory authority:

Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)

  • Website: www.ico.org.uk
  • Helpline: 0303 123 1113
  • Address: Wycliffe House, Water Lane, Wilmslow, Cheshire, SK9 5AF

9.9 How to Exercise Your Rights

You can exercise any of the above rights by:

  • Emailing us at privacy@thegoodtech.com
  • Using the Privacy Request Form available in your account settings (if you have an account)

We may ask you to verify your identity before processing your request. We will respond within 30 days, and will notify you if additional time is needed.

11. Security

We apply appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, accidental loss, alteration, or destruction. These include:

  • HTTPS/TLS encryption for all data transmitted between your browser and our servers
  • Password hashing using bcrypt — we never store passwords in plain text
  • Role-based access control limiting data access to staff who need it for their role
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments
  • Incident response procedures for identifying and managing personal data breaches, including notifying the ICO within 72 hours where required

Despite these measures, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is completely secure. If you suspect a security incident involving your data, please contact us at security@thegoodtech.com.
